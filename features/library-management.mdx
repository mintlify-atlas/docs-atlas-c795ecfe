Kafka's Library feature helps you organize and access your favorite items and downloads. Save items you love, manage your downloads, and quickly resume your recent reading and listening.

## Accessing Your Library

Your Library is available from the bottom navigation bar:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Library">
    Tap the Library icon in the bottom navigation to open your personal library.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose a tab">
    The Library has two tabs: "Favorites" and "Downloads". Swipe or tap to switch between them.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your Library content syncs across devices when you're signed in, so your favorites are available everywhere.
</Note>

## Favorites

Save items you want to keep track of or read later:

### Adding Favorites

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find an item">
    Navigate to any item detail page from search results or the homepage.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tap the favorite button">
    Look for the heart icon in the top bar or action buttons on the item detail page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm addition">
    The heart icon fills to indicate the item is now in your favorites.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Viewing Favorites

Access your saved items from the Favorites tab:

- **Grid view** - See items in a compact grid with cover art
- **List view** - View items in a detailed list with metadata
- **Switch layouts** - Toggle between grid and list using the layout button at the top

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Grid View" icon="th">
    Displays 2 columns on phones, 4 columns on tablets. Perfect for browsing by cover art.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="List View" icon="list">
    Shows detailed information including title, creator, and description. Better for searching specific items.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Removing Favorites

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the item">
    Tap any favorite item to open its detail page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tap the favorite button">
    Tap the filled heart icon to remove the item from your favorites.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm removal">
    The heart icon becomes unfilled, and the item is removed from your favorites list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use favorites to create your personal collection of items you want to read, listen to, or reference later.
</Tip>

## Downloads

Manage all your downloaded content in one place:

### Viewing Downloads

The Downloads tab shows all items you've downloaded:

- **Download status** - See which files are downloading, paused, or completed
- **File information** - View file name, size, and format
- **Quick access** - Tap any downloaded item to open it immediately

### Download Status Indicators

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Downloading" icon="spinner">
    Shows progress percentage and a pause button. Files are being downloaded.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Completed" icon="check-circle">
    Green checkmark indicates file is downloaded and ready to read/listen offline.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Paused" icon="pause-circle">
    Download is paused. Tap to resume downloading.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Failed" icon="exclamation-circle">
    Download failed. Tap to retry.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Managing Downloaded Files

Control your downloads directly from the Downloads tab:

- **Open file** - Tap a completed download to open it in the reader/player
- **Pause download** - Tap the pause button on active downloads
- **Resume download** - Tap paused downloads to continue
- **Retry failed** - Tap failed downloads to try again

### Download Location

Customize where files are saved on your device:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find location button">
    At the top of the Downloads tab, you'll see the current download location displayed.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Change location">
    Tap the location button to open your device's folder picker.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select folder">
    Choose a folder on your device or SD card where you want downloads saved.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm selection">
    The new location is saved and future downloads will go there.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Changing download location doesn't move existing files. Only new downloads will use the new location.
</Note>

### Storage Information

Kafka provides helpful storage information:

- **Total download size** - See how much space your downloads use
- **Storage prompt** - Get notified when storage is running low
- **Individual file sizes** - Each download shows its file size

<Tip>
  Monitor your download storage to ensure you have space for new content. Consider removing finished items you won't revisit.
</Tip>

## Recent Items

Quickly access items you've recently read or listened to:

### Continue Reading Section

On the homepage, find the "Continue Reading" section:

- Shows items you've recently accessed
- Displays your progress (current page or playback position)
- Tap to resume from where you left off
- Swipe to remove items you're done with

### Recent Items Page

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open all recent items">
    Tap "See All" in the Continue Reading section on the homepage.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Browse your history">
    View a complete list of items you've recently accessed, ordered by recency.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Manage items">
    Remove items from recent history by swiping or using the remove button.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Card title="Automatic History" icon="history">
  Kafka automatically tracks items you open. No manual action needed - just start reading or listening, and items appear in your recent list.
</Card>

## Syncing Across Devices

When signed in, your library syncs automatically:

### What Syncs

- **Favorites** - Your favorite items are available on all devices
- **Reading positions** - Resume from the same position on any device
- **Recent items** - Your reading history syncs across devices

### What Doesn't Sync

- **Downloaded files** - Downloads are stored locally on each device
- **Download location** - Each device has its own download folder setting

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in">
    Log in with the same account on multiple devices.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Automatic sync">
    Favorites and reading positions sync automatically when connected to the internet.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Access anywhere">
    Open Kafka on any device to see your favorites and continue from your last position.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  **Sign in required:** You must be signed in to sync library data. Anonymous users have local-only library data.
</Note>

## Empty States

When you first start using Kafka:

### No Favorites Yet

The Favorites tab shows:
- Message: "No favorites items yet"
- Prompts you to explore and add items

### No Downloads Yet

The Downloads tab shows:
- Message: "No downloads yet"
- Instructions to download items for offline access

### Sign-In Prompt

If not signed in, you'll see:
- "Log in to sync favorites" message
- Tap to go to the login screen
- Sign in to enable cloud sync

<Tip>
  Create an account to unlock syncing and never lose your library data when switching devices.
</Tip>
