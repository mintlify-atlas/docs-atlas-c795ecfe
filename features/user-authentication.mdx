Kafka offers flexible authentication options to suit your preferences. Sign in with Google, create an account with email, or use the app anonymously. Signing in enables cross-device syncing of favorites and reading progress.

## Sign In Options

Kafka provides three ways to use the app:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Google Sign In" icon="google">
    Quick sign-in using your Google account. Automatic authentication with no password to remember.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Email & Password" icon="envelope">
    Create an account with your email address and a secure password you choose.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Anonymous Use" icon="user-secret">
    Use the app without signing in. All features work, but data stays on your device only.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Why Sign In?

Creating an account unlocks syncing features:

### Benefits of Signing In

- **Sync favorites** - Access your favorite items on all devices
- **Resume anywhere** - Reading positions sync across devices
- **Preserve history** - Recent items available everywhere
- **Backup data** - Never lose your library if you change devices
- **Recommendations** - Get personalized suggestions based on your reading

### Anonymous Mode

You can use Kafka without signing in:
- All reading and listening features work
- Downloads work normally
- Data stored locally on device only
- No syncing across devices
- Data lost if app is uninstalled

<Note>
  You can sign in later without losing data. Your local favorites and history will merge with your cloud account.
</Note>

## Signing In with Google

The fastest way to get started:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open sign-in screen">
    Tap "Sign In" from the profile page or the prompt in your Library.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Google sign-in">
    Tap the "Continue with Google" button.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose account">
    Select your Google account from the list or add a new account.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Grant permissions">
    Allow Kafka to access your basic profile information (name and email).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Start using Kafka">
    You're signed in! Your favorites and progress will now sync automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Google Sign-In Security

- Uses OAuth 2.0 for secure authentication
- Kafka never sees your Google password
- Only basic profile information is accessed
- You can revoke access anytime from Google account settings

<Tip>
  Google Sign-In is the quickest option and doesn't require remembering another password.
</Tip>

## Creating an Email Account

Sign up with email and password:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open sign-up">
    From the sign-in screen, tap "New User? Signup here" at the bottom.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter email">
    Type your email address in the email field.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create password">
    Enter a secure password. Requirements:
    - At least 6 characters
    - Combination of letters and numbers recommended
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm signup">
    Tap the "Sign Up" button to create your account.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify email">
    Check your email for a verification link and click it to activate your account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Email Requirements

- Must be a valid email address
- Each email can only be used once
- Used for password resets and account recovery
- Not shared with third parties

<Note>
  **Keep your password safe:** Store your password in a password manager or somewhere secure. You'll need it to sign in on other devices.
</Note>

## Signing In with Email

Return to your account on any device:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open login screen">
    Tap "Sign In" from the profile page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter credentials">
    Type your registered email and password.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Sign in">
    Tap the "Login" button to access your account.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Syncing begins">
    Your favorites and reading progress sync automatically after signing in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Forgot Password

If you can't remember your password:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap forgot password">
    On the login screen, tap "Forgot password?" below the password field.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter email">
    Type the email address associated with your account.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check email">
    Look for a password reset email from Kafka.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Reset password">
    Click the reset link in the email and create a new password.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Sign in">
    Return to Kafka and sign in with your new password.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Check your spam folder if you don't receive the password reset email within a few minutes.
</Tip>

## Data Syncing

Understand how your data syncs:

### What Syncs

When signed in, these items sync automatically:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Favorites" icon="heart">
    All items you've favorited appear on all signed-in devices.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Reading Progress" icon="book-bookmark">
    Current page/position for each item syncs across devices.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Recent Items" icon="clock-rotate-left">
    Your reading history stays consistent everywhere.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Preferences" icon="sliders">
    Some app preferences sync (like reader settings).
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### What Doesn't Sync

- **Downloaded files** - Downloads are device-specific
- **Download location** - Each device has its own setting
- **Recent searches** - Search history is local only
- **Playback queue** - Audio queues don't sync

### Sync Timing

- **Automatic** - Syncing happens in the background
- **Immediate** - Changes upload within seconds
- **On launch** - Full sync when you open the app
- **Requires internet** - Syncing needs an active connection

<Tip>
  If syncing seems delayed, check your internet connection and try pulling down to refresh on the Library page.
</Tip>

Your data is protected:

### Data Protection

- **Secure storage** - Cloud data stored with industry-standard encryption

## Troubleshooting Sign-In

Resolve common authentication issues:

### Can't Sign In

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Wrong Password" icon="key">
    - Use "Forgot password" to reset
    - Check for typos
    - Try typing password in notes app first
    - Ensure CAPS LOCK is off
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Google Sign-In Fails" icon="google">
    - Check Google Play Services is updated
    - Try selecting account again
    - Sign out of Google and back in
    - Verify internet connection
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Account Not Found" icon="user-slash">
    - Verify email address is correct
    - Check if you signed up with Google instead
    - Try alternate email addresses
    - Create new account if needed
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Email Not Verified" icon="envelope-open-text">
    - Check email inbox and spam
    - Request new verification email
    - Wait a few minutes and try again
    - Ensure email address is correct
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Syncing Issues

If your data isn't syncing:

1. **Verify sign-in** - Ensure you're signed into the same account on all devices
2. **Check internet** - Syncing requires active internet connection
3. **Force sync** - Pull to refresh on Library page
4. **Sign out and in** - Try signing out and back in to force sync
5. **Wait a moment** - Give sync a few seconds to complete

<Tip>
  Most sign-in issues are resolved by ensuring you're using the correct email address and password, and that you have a stable internet connection.
</Tip>

## Best Practices

Get the most from Kafka authentication:

- **Sign in early** - Create an account when you first start using Kafka to enable syncing from the beginning
- **Use strong passwords** - If using email signup, choose a secure, unique password
- **Keep email current** - Use an email address you regularly check for recovery
- **Sign in on all devices** - Use the same account on all your devices to keep everything in sync
- **Stay signed in** - No need to sign out unless sharing your device

<Card title="Recommended: Google Sign-In" icon="check-circle">
  For most users, Google Sign-In provides the best experience: it's quick, secure, and doesn't require remembering another password.
</Card>
